‚öôÔ∏è General

  • When drawing lines, hold down shift to draw at perfect angles. This is good for drawing vertical or horizontal lines, or for drawing parallel lines.
  • When drawing lines, your pointer will snap to intersections and points to help you draw the graph easier.
  • Arrows and steps are not graded, but you can use them if you wish to help organize your graph.
  • Points are also not graded, but lines can snap to them, which makes it easier to create intersections.
  • The grader may not be 100% perfect all of the time! There are still a few bugs here and there.

üè∑Ô∏è Labeling Guide

Use standard IB terms ‚Äî not case-sensitive:

  • Axes: Price ($), Quantity (of ____), Real GDP, Price Level
  • Lines: D, S, LRAS, SRAS, AD, MSC, MPB, etc.
  • Points: Used for guidance only, not assessed
  • Dotted-line labels: P, P1, Q, Q1, Y, Y1, etc.
  • The grader does not distinguish between p and pe or q and qe, but it will distinguish between other forms of equilibrium, such as W and We.
  • If you don't know what the grader wants a certain curve or axis to be labeled, usually the grader will tell you what lines you are missing in its feedback.
